The GPA calculator requires two data inputs for each subject: the credit hours and the credit grades (or credit scores). The credit hours represent the weight or importance of the course, typically assigned by your educational institution. The credit grades or scores are the numerical or letter grades you have earned in each course. Knowing the credit hours for your courses, let's move on to understanding the credit grades.

To find your credit grades, you need to determine the percentage score for each subject. This percentage is usually available on your report card or transcript. Once you have the percentage score for a subject, you can convert it to a credit grade using the standard grading scale provided by your institution. For example, a percentage score of 85% might correspond to an 'A' grade, which might be equivalent to 4.0 on a 4.0 scale. Record the credit grades for each subject as you will need them for calculating your GPA.
